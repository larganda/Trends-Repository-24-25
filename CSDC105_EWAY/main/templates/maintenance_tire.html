Tire Maintenance Guide

Maintaining your tires is essential for safety and performance. Here's how to keep them in good condition:

Tire Pressure:

  • Check regularly: Use a tire gauge to check the pressure before each ride.
  • Recommended PSI: Inflate the tires to the PSI recommended on the tire sidewall (usually between 30-60 PSI depending on the tire type).
  • Avoid under/over-inflation: Low pressure can cause flats, and high pressure can lead to poor grip and an uncomfortable ride.

Tire Inspection:

  • Check for wear and tear: Inspect for cuts, punctures, or excessive wear on the tread. Replace tires when the tread is low (usually below 1.5 mm).
  • Inspect sidewalls: Look for cracks or bulges, as they indicate potential safety issues.

Cleaning and Protection:

  • Clean tires: Wash off dirt and debris with mild soap and a soft brush.
  • Consider puncture protection: Use puncture-resistant tires or tire liners to prevent flats, and apply sealant for added protection.
